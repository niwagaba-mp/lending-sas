INTERNAL AUDIT REPORT 
Lends-Up Financial Services Ltd 
 
Auditee Tumumanye Oscar 
Position Loans Officer 
Branch Entebbe – Abaita Ababiri Trading Centre 
Audit Period February 2026 – May 2026 
Date of Absconding 4th May 2026 
Report Date 10 May 2026 
 
1. Background 
Tumumanye Oscar was employed by Lendsup Financial Services Ltd as a Loans Officer 
stationed at the Entebbe – Abaita Ababiri Trading Centre branch. His duties included 
recruiting clients for loans, inspecting client businesses to minimize default ris ks, 
assisting clients in completing loan application forms, disbursing loan amounts, 
collecting daily loan repayments, reporting collections to the office cashier, and preparing 
daily reports. 
2. Audit Findings 
Following a review of loan transactions, client documentation, cash disbursement 
records, repayment records, and client verification exercises conducted for the period 
February 2026 to May 2026, the following findings were established: 
 The Loans Officer, M r. Tumumanye Oscar, abused his position and access to the 
company loan processing system to fraudulently process loans for personal 
benefit.  
 Loan application forms relating to several investigated accounts were found to 
contain forged and/or falsified client information.  
 A number of loan accounts were processed in the names of clients who, upon 
verification, denied having applied for or received the respective loans.  
 Several telephone contacts captured on the investigated loan application forms 
were found to be invalid, unreachable, or falsely recorded.  
--- Page 2 ---
 Audit verification established that in certain instances, loan amounts processed 
and disbursed exceeded the amounts allegedly requested by the purported clients.  
 Physical field verification conducted by the branch supervisor confirmed that a 
number of previous clients had completed their earlier loan obligations and had 
not applied for subsequent loans reflected in the system.  
 Review of repayment records further revealed instances where client repayments  
collected by the Loans Officer were not fully reported and/or remitted to the 
office cashier as required under company procedures.  
 During the course of the field verification and audit investigations, Mr. 
Tumumanye Oscar absconded from duty on 4th May 20 26 without authorization 
and failed to return to work thereafter.  
 Based on the evidence obtained through document examination, cash -out 
verification, telephone interviews, field investigations, and repayment 
reconciliation, the audit established reasonabl e grounds to conclude that 
fraudulent ghost loans were intentionally created and processed by the Loans 
Officer.  
 The audit established a total financial loss exposure of UGX 16,139,000 affecting 
92 client accounts. 
3. Audit Process 
 Loans issued between February 2026 and May 2026 were subjected to audit 
verification. 
 Cash-out audits were conducted to verify loan disbursement authenticity. 
 Phone call confirmations were made to clients listed under the investigated loans. 
 Most phone numbers provided during loan application processing were 
unreachable or invalid. 
 Clients who were successfully contacted confirmed that they had neither applied 
for nor received the investigated loans. 
 The branch supervisor was instructed to carry out physical field verification of the 
affected clients. 
--- Page 3 ---
 Field verification confirmed that several old clients had completed their previous 
loans and had not requested new loans. 
 During the field verification exercise, the Loans Officer absconded from duty  on 
4th May 2026. 
4. Audit Conclusion 
Based on the audit evidence gathered, it was concluded that Mr. Tumumanye Oscar 
intentionally and fra udulently processed ghost  loans and diverted company funds for 
personal use. The investigation also established that in some instances, he failed to report 
client repayment s to the office. The total confirmed financial loss attributable to the 
fraudulent activities amounts to UGX 16,139,000 affecting 92 clients. 
5. Recommendations 
1. Immediate recovery efforts should be initiated through police intervention and/or 
mediation to recover the stolen funds. 
2. Field officers should no longer directly disburse loan funds to clients without 
supervision. 
3. Managers and supervisors should escort field officers during loan disbursement 
exercises or require clients to collect funds directly from the office. 
4. Surprise field audits should be conducted regularly to strengthen accountability. 
5. Frequent phone call verifications should be carried out for both new and existing 
clients. 
6. Special field audits should be undertaken for clients whose phone numbers fail to go 
through. 
7. Clients should receive automated SMS notifications upon loan repayment and loan 
disbursement. 
8. Clients should be sensitized to always contact the office directly for loan applications, 
balances, and payment confirmations.

1) Executive Summary 
Internal Audit conducted an investigation into suspected fraud, theft, ghost lending, and  
operational misconduct involving Mr. Kagina William , a Loans Officer at Lends Up Financial 
Services Ltd, Luzira Branch, located at Luzira Trading Centre, Nakawa–Kampala. 
The investigation established that Mr. Kagina William intentionally failed to deliver the approved 
loan funds to the rightful and to remit f ield loan repayments collected from clients and instead 
converted the funds for personal use, resulting in a confirmed shortage of UGX 14,250,000  
affecting 67 clients. These actions caused severe financial and em otional distress to clients who 
depended on the loans for medical treatment, business operations, and family welfare. 
During the course of the audit investigation, Mr. Kagina William absconded from duty on 11 
May 2026 , an act interpreted as de liberate avoidance of accountability and ongoing audit 
procedures. 
The investigation additionally identified serious supervisory negligence involving Mr. 
Kamugisha Alex , the Branch Supervisor, who failed to execute his oversight responsibilities 
despite reportedly escorting the Loans Officer to the field on a daily basis. Evidence indicates that 
the ghost loans were issued in his presence and under his supervision. Mr. Kamugisha Alex further 
absconded from work on 12 May 2026 after being instructed to accom pany the field auditor for 
reinspection and client verification exercises.

2) Background and Scope of Audit 
2.1 Background 
Lends Up Financial Services Ltd operates a field-based microfinance lending system where Loans 
Officers are responsible for recruiting clients, assessing businesses, processing loan applications, 
collecting repayments, and submitting both repayments and documentation to the office. 
Supervisors are expected to oversee field operations, verify loan activities, ensure compliance, and 
monitor the conduct of Loans Officers. 
2.2 Scope of Audit 
The audit covered the following areas during the period 1st April 2026 to 12th May 2026: 
 Loan repayment collection and remittance records 
 Loan application documentation and approval procedures 
 Client verification and existence confirmation 
 Loan disbursement procedures 
 Ghost loan identification and portfolio review 
 Field supervision and management oversight 
 Financial loss and reputational impact assessment

of: 
UGX 14,250,000 (Fourteen Million Two Hundred Fifty Thousand Shillings Only)

1. INTRODUCTION 
This report presents the findings of an  audit conducted at Leeway Financial Services Limited 
Nyendo Branch, focusing specifically on the conduct, cash handling, and transactional integrity of 
the branch cashier, Ms. Kwera Dorah. 
The audit was commissioned following persistent cash shortages, unbalanced daily reports, and 
suspected fraudulent activities within the branch. The purpose of this audit was to establish the 
existence, extent, nature, and causes of the losses incurred, identify responsible parties, and 
provide recommendations suitable for management action. 
2. AUDIT SCOPE AND METHODOLOGY 
2.1 Scope 
The audit covered the period June 2025 to October 2025 and focused on: 
 Cash-in and cash-out transactions 
 Client cash-out records 
 Daily cashier reconciliation reports 
 Loan disbursement and repayment records 
 Client registers and supporting documentation 
2.2 Methodology 
The audit was conducted through: 
 Physical inspection of cash records and registers 
 Reconciliation of daily cash reports against system records 
 Review of office client cash-out and field visitations for confirmation 
 Verification of client details, phone numbers, and signatures 
 Analytical review of shortages and transaction patterns

 Name: Kwera Dorah 
 Position: Cashier
